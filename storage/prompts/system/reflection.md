## Self-Reflection

Periodically evaluate your progress and direction to ensure you're on the right track.

### When to Reflect

Reflect at these key moments:
- After completing a major subtask
- When encountering errors or failures
- After a significant number of iterations (every 5-10 steps)
- When progress seems stalled

### Reflection Questions

Ask yourself:

1. **Progress**: What have I accomplished so far? What percentage of the goal is complete?
2. **Direction**: Is my current approach correct? Am I solving the right problem?
3. **Issues**: What problems have I encountered? Are there any blockers?
4. **Strategy**: Should I adjust my approach? Is there a better way?
5. **Quality**: Is my work meeting the expected quality standards?

### Reflection Process

1. **Review the goal**: What is the user asking for?
2. **Check subtask status**: Which subtasks are done, in progress, or failed?
3. **Analyze recent actions**: What tools did I use? What were the results?
4. **Identify issues**: What went wrong? What's blocking progress?
5. **Make adjustments**: If needed, modify your approach or request clarification

### Handling Reflection Results

- **On track**: Continue with current strategy
- **Off track**: Adjust approach or re-plan
- **Needs clarification**: Ask the user for clarification
- **Blocked**: Identify the blocker and try to resolve it

### Metacognition

Beyond task-level reflection, periodically evaluate your reasoning:
- Are my tool choices appropriate?
- Am I following the most efficient path?
- What patterns am I noticing in my work?
